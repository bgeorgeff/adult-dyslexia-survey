Before you start

This screener looks for patterns often linked with dyslexia in adults. It can't diagnose dyslexia — only a qualified professional can do that. There are no right or wrong answers, and your responses stay on your device. Answer based on your everyday experience, and take your time.

A bit of background
Has anyone in your close family — a parent, brother or sister, or your own child — been diagnosed with dyslexia, or found reading and spelling difficult? (Optional)
Reading History and Habits
As a child, learning to read felt much harder for me than it did for my classmates.
I read more slowly than most people I know.
When I have a choice, I avoid reading long passages of text.
Sounding Out Words
I find it hard to sound out an unfamiliar word, even when I can see it written.
I mix up similar-sounding words (for example, "specific" and "Pacific").
I have trouble breaking long words into syllables in my head.
I find it hard to play with the sounds in a word — like saying it without one of its sounds, or swapping the sounds around.
Visually Similar Words and Spelling
I sometimes confuse words that look alike, such as "form" and "from", or "trail" and "trial".
When I write, I leave out small words like "the" or "is" without noticing.
I write a word and it looks wrong, but I can't tell what's wrong with it.
Word Retrieval and Naming
I know what I want to say, but the word I'm reaching for won't come to me.
I sometimes call an object by the wrong name (e.g., "fridge" instead of "oven").
I struggle to remember the names of people I've recently met, even when I remember their faces.
Working Memory and Sequencing
When someone gives me a phone number or short list out loud, I have trouble holding onto it long enough to write it down.
Following multi-step verbal instructions is difficult for me.
I lose my place when reading aloud or reading down a column of numbers.
Written Expression and Organization
Putting my thoughts in order on paper is much harder than saying them out loud.
I rely heavily on spellcheck, autocorrect, or someone else to proofread what I write.
My handwriting is hard to read, even for me.
Time and Direction
I mix up left and right, even as an adult.
I have trouble judging how much time has passed or how long a task will take.
I get lost easily, or I find directions like "go north" or "take the third left" hard to follow.

The research behind this screener

This screener is not a copy of any single test — every question was written from scratch. The areas it covers reflect what decades of research most consistently link to dyslexia in adults: early reading history, phonological (sound) processing, spelling and visual word confusion, word retrieval and naming, verbal working memory, written expression, and left/right and time awareness. We include a family-history question because dyslexia is strongly hereditary.
